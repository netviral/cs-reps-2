## Overview  
The **Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)** by EC-Council is one of the most widely recognized cybersecurity credentials in the world. It equips you with the skills and mindset of a professional hacker — but for ethical purposes — teaching you how to legally identify and patch security vulnerabilities in systems and networks.

---

## Recommended Background  
Students who have completed the following courses with strong grades (A or A–) are best suited to pursue CEH:  
- **Information Security (InfoSec)**  
- **Computer Networks (CN)**  
- **Cryptography**  
- **System Security and Privacy**

These courses build the theoretical and practical foundation necessary for CEH topics like system scanning, enumeration, and exploitation.

---

## Skills You’ll Gain  
- Footprinting and reconnaissance  
- System hacking and privilege escalation  
- Malware threats and analysis  
- Web application security  
- Wireless network security  
- Cloud and IoT security  
- Incident handling and reporting  

---

## Exam Details  
- **Format:** 125 Multiple-Choice Questions  
- **Duration:** 4 Hours  
- **Mode:** Online (remote proctoring available) or at authorized testing centers  
- **Passing Score:** Varies between 60%–85% depending on the question bank  

---

## Fees and Validity  
- **Exam Fee:** Approx. **$1,200 USD (₹1,00,000 INR)**  
- **Certification Validity:** **3 years** (renewable through EC-Council’s Continuing Education credits)

## Career Outlook  
Holding a CEH certification opens up roles such as:  
- **Penetration Tester**  
- **Security Analyst**  
- **Incident Responder**  
- **Vulnerability Researcher**  
- **Cybersecurity Consultant**

Professionals with CEH are often employed by security firms, consulting organizations, and government agencies to test and fortify digital systems.

---

## Why It Matters  
CEH is not just about hacking — it’s about understanding systems at a deep level to **build stronger defenses**.  
For CS students, it bridges **theory from courses** like *InfoSec* and *Cryptography* with **real-world application**, making it a perfect step toward a career in ethical hacking or information security.
